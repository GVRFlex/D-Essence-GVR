Chemical Peels

A controlled, clinical exfoliation that renews the surface and sparks healthy repair—revealing a smoother, brighter, more even complexion. Depth and agent are selected to your skin, goals, and downtime.

Overview

A chemical peel applies a calibrated solution to gently dissolve targeted layers of skin. This controlled process sheds dull surface cells, stimulates renewal, and supports collagen remodeling—improving texture, tone, and the look of fine lines, discoloration, and shallow acne scars.

We offer light, medium, and select deep-strength protocols. Your provider will match the agent and depth to your skin type, history, and goals, balancing results with recovery time.

Common benefits

  • Smoother texture
  • Brighter, more even tone
  • Softens fine lines
  • Fades sun spots
  • Helps melasma (selected cases)
  • Refines shallow acne scars

Outcomes depend on peel depth, skin type, and adherence to aftercare and sun protection.

What to expect

Every peel begins with consultation and skin prep guidance. We’ll align on goals, timeline, and recovery before selecting your formula and strength.

Before your visit
  • Share medical history, photosensitivity, and recent treatments.
  • Protect from sun; pause strong actives per guidance.
  • Arrive with clean skin (no heavy makeup).
During treatment
  • Solution applied in passes; “tingle/heat” is common.
  • Agent may be neutralized or left to self-terminate per protocol.
  • Session time ~20–45 minutes.
Recovery timeline
  • Light peels: pinkness and micro-flaking for a few days.
  • Medium: visible peeling over several days as new skin emerges.
  • Deep: more downtime and strict aftercare; results unfold over weeks.

Aftercare

  • Do not pick, rub, or prematurely peel flaking skin.
  • Use gentle cleanser and soothing, fragrance-free moisturizer.
  • Broad-spectrum SPF daily; minimize direct sun.
  • Avoid strenuous heat/exercise, steam, and saunas early on.
  • Pause strong acids/retinoids until cleared by your provider.
  • Follow any prescribed topicals (e.g., antiviral) if indicated.

Safety & considerations

Common, temporary effects
  • Redness, warmth, tightness
  • Flaking or visible peel (by depth)
  • Sun sensitivity—SPF is mandatory
Less common risks
  • Temporary pigment shift (more caution for deeper tones)
  • Infection or delayed healing if aftercare lapses
  • Scarring with excessive depth or poor technique (rare)
Not medical advice. Your plan is personalized to skin tone, history, and lifestyle to minimize risk.

Who may need to wait or avoid

  • Active infection, open wounds, or inflamed acne in the area
  • Recent isotretinoin use (timing per provider)
  • History of abnormal scarring or keloids
  • Photosensitizing medications

Additional precautions

  • Pregnancy or nursing (case-by-case)
  • History of cold sores—consider antiviral prophylaxis
  • Deeper skin tones require conservative protocols
  • Strict SPF and sun avoidance are essential

FAQ

How many sessions will I need?

It depends on depth and goals. Some see significant improvement with a single medium peel, while others prefer a series of light peels for ongoing maintenance.

When will I see results?

Light peels show glow within days; medium and deeper peels reveal smoother, clearer skin as flaking resolves and new skin surfaces over days to weeks.

Is it painful?

Most feel tingling or warmth during application. Medium or deeper peels can use topical anesthesia and careful monitoring for comfort.

Can I combine peels with other treatments?

Yes—many pair peels with microneedling, light lasers, or skincare. We’ll coordinate timing to protect the barrier and maximize results.
